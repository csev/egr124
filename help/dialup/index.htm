How to make a new connection for Windows 95

  1. First locate the "My Computer" icon somewhere on your desktop and double click on it.

  2. You should see something like this.  Double click on the "Dial-Up Networking" file, and a window like the one below should appear.









    3.   Double click on "Make New Connection," and the window below should pop up.




     

    4.  You should then see the following window.  Your modem should already be displayed in the "Select a modem:" space.  If it is not click on the triangle to the left and highlight your modem.  If your modem still is not listed you may not have your modem set up.  You must do this first.  See your modem's, and Windows 95's instructions on how to setup your modem.  When you are done click "Next."



    5.  Type in the number for your local MichNet access number or your service provider and click on the triangle to select your country.  Now click "Next."









 

6.  Now click on "Finish" and you are ready to go.  When you start up Netscape the automatic dialer should start up.  If it doesn't you can always click on "My Computer" and then click on Dial-Up Networking (same as step 1 and 2).  You should see the connection you just made.  Click on it to connect to MichNet.  You should see the window below.






7.  Type in your pilot user name with the @msu attached at the end like in the image to the left.  The password is also the same as your pilot password.  If you are not using MichNet you will use your user name and password that your service provider gives you, and you may be required to do additional setup procedures.  Check with the documentation your provider sent.  The phone number displayed should be the same number as you used to create the connection earlier. Next you need to click on the "Dial Properties..." button, and the window below should appear.



8.  You may want to change the name of the location you are dialing from, especially if you will be taking your computer back to East Lansing This Fall, otherwise you can use the default name.  To create a new location, click on "New" and enter a name.  Next, type in the area code for this location.  Now fill out the rest of the areas that apply to you.  About call waiting, if you do have calling waiting it is important to disable it before dialing up to MichNet.  If call waiting is left on and another call comes in while you are online you will lose the connection.  To disable call waiting, click on the triangle and select the appropriate code for your area.  If you are unsure what code to use check a local phone book or contact your operator.  If you are choosing to call an access number which is long distance check to see if there is a local access number.  If not, you may want to consider using a service provider who does have a local access number since the cost of connecting to a service might be cheaper than paying long distance rates.

Good Luck!
