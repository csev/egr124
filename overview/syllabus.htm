Student Responsibilities and Other Course Information

  • Course Objectives: The objective of this course is to learn about the Internet and the tools to use the Internet. A further goal of the class is to understand how to best teach a class using the Internet.
  • Course Dates: The course will begin officially January 14, 1998 and end May 8, 1998 All work must be completed by May 8, 1998.
  • First Week of Class (or before class starts): Students will be required to find a location to view and/or listen to the lectures. This may require installation of software on a home computer or finding an appropriately equipped microlab. If the students cannot view the lectures by January 21, 1998 they will be dropped from the class.
  • Pacing: This is not a completely modular self-paced class. Students should not expect to complete the class during the first few weeks, nor should they expect to wait to the last minute and complete all the work in the last two weeks of the semester. In order to accomodate some flexibility in schedules, the lowest homework score will be dropped in the computation of the homework grade. Students unable to complete the Mid-term or final exam at the appointed time must make arrangements with the instructor in advance just like in a non-virtual class.
  • Attendance: Students will be expected to attend each lecture across the Internet. There will be a time window for each lecture for attendance purposes. Attendance will be taken using the world-wide-web.
  • Class Participation: During the class, students will be expected to participate in several activities. First, there will be a class-wide conferencing system with topic areas. After each lecture, each student wil be required to comment on that lecture in the designated conference area. There will be several class meetings throughout the semester in the chat room. Students will be required to attend at least two of the class meetings.
  • On-line Homework and Exams: Homework and Exams will be administered on-line using the CAPA system. While it is acceptable to discuss homework with other people in an effort to better learn the material, students are to work on the exams with absolutely no help from others. By taking this class you are agreeing to perform the exams on-line without assistance.
  • Continuous Quality Improvement: This course and its materials can always be improved. The instructor invites and encourages comment on any and all aspects of the course. A portion of the class participation grade will be based on the positive and negative feedback which each student gives regarding the course. This feedback can take many forms. After each lecture, each student is asked to rate the lecture and comment on how the material could be improved.
  • Homework Questions: Most homework sets will be available on-line for two weeks. As an incentive for students to begin working on the homework early, during the first week, students who feel that a question is vague or feel that their answer is wrong, can send E-Mail to the instructor identifying which question they felt was a problem, and including their rational why the question has a problem. The insturctor may revise the question or simply give the student credit for their answer.
  • Communication: Most of the documentation for the course will be made available through the World-Wide-Web and through E-Mail. Students will be expected to use E-Mail regularly and class announcements will be done using E-Mail. Any course announcements will be mailed to the Pilot accounts of the students. Students who do not read their E-Mail regularly (at least 3-4 times per week) on Pilot should either forward their Pilot account to an active mail site or check the the class web page 3-4 times per week. If you do not have a Pilot account, you must send the instructor your E-Mail address during the first week of the semester.
  • Acceptable Use: All students are expected to follow the MSU Acceptable use policy available non-line at http://www.msu.edu/dig/aup/msuaup.html
  • Honors Options: Students interested in pursuing an honors option should contact the instructor.
