The Internet as an Instructional Tool

Since the beginning of formalized schooling, teachers have tried to incorporate new technologies into learning. From simple tools like slide projectors and calculators to the newest items such as computers. The most advanced and emerging teaching tool is the internet. While many teachers use it to give students access to information, some instructors are using it as a virtual classroom. The introduction of such a new medium leads to questions of the positive and negative aspects.

There are many benefits to this type of instruction. The wide access to the internet now prevalent would perhaps allow more students to take a course. Computer Science 291 is an excellent example. A centralized class is presented and people from around Michigan and the world are able to access the information and study the material. I have benefited from this myself. Since I spend my summers in Holland, two hours from the classrooms of Michigan State, I would not be able to take a course like this unless it were offered on the internet. This also leads to the ability to choose from where one logs onto the classroom. If a student is on vacation in a traditional class they would miss the class and have to obtain notes from another student. If a student is on vacation in the internet based course, they may be able to access the information and keep up with the class. Overall, a major benefit of internet based teaching is the flexibility of location.

The flexibility to scheduling is another benefit of this medium of teaching. The students have greater ability to decide when to access information and lectures. Usually a class is offered two to three times a week, at a set time. An internet based course can be accessed any time of the day or night. If a student has to work during the day, the materials will be available after they finish. Also, a student can work at their own pace, repeating lectures and going over material until they are sure they understand it. This may help students who have experienced professors who lecture too quickly!

Another benefit of web based teaching is the professor’s ability to ensure complete and uniform delivery of his or her message. In a large or small traditional lecture, a teacher’s message could be interpreted many different ways. Sometimes what one person hears is not the same message or information another person hears. The internet based teaching allows the teachers to pin point what they feel are the important items. It also allows them to ensure that the message presented is the same for everyone. This message would also be available for change and update as the information changes. Many of the classes offered online are technical in nature. Since the technical fields are rapidly evolving, information may be outdated from day to day. The ability to alter information quickly and easily is a definite benefit!

While there are many benefits of internet based teaching, there are also negative points as well. There is an obvious lack of human contact. In a traditional teaching classroom, the professor is available right away. While on the internet a teacher may take a little longer to reach by email or phone. Students may also have trouble picking up certain items and not be able to ask questions immediately, with immediate feedback. While there are others, I personally think this is the most prevalent and obvious.

Overall, I think that internet based teaching is a terrific way to offer a course. I really have benefited from my classes on the internet and think that other students have as well. It really is the newest, most innovative way to learn!

Emilie Curtis, 1997
